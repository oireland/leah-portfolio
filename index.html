Philosophy

I am a RIBA Part 1 Architecture graduate dedicated to creating spaces that restore and inspire. My design philosophy is anchored in neuroarchitecture, exploring how the built environment impacts our mental and physical well being.

Driven by my dissertation research looking beyond grey, I advocate for moving past sterile aesthetics by using intentional color theory and natural elements to design spaces that actively improve human life. My personal style is simple yet highly effective, artistic, and deeply human centric.

Approach

As a designer, I bridge the gap between technical precision and emotional atmosphere. I utilize AutoCAD for rigorous drafting and SketchUp for spatial modeling, but my true passion lies in the art of the hand. I rely heavily on fineliner and watercolor sketching to bring the raw atmosphere and narrative of a project to life.
